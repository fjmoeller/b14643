Ceres-1
 
 

Galactic Energy Aerospace Technology Co.


Galactic Energy Aerospace Technology, established in February 2018, a further Chinese commercial launch company, is aiming for a March 2020 test flight of its "Ceres-1" solid launcher from Jiuquan, advertising 79 kg of remaining payload for launch to 97.4°, 500 km SSO.
The Ceres-1 (or Gushengxing) launch vehicle will consist of three solid stages and a liquid-propellant fourth stage, capable of placing 350 kg in a 200 km LEO and 230 kg to 200 km SSO. Ceres-1 is of 1.4 m diameter, 19 m tall and 30 tons launch wight.

Note: For an analysis of the Ceres-1 variants, see "Versions"

 

2022:
A new, white-painted Ceres-1 (Y3) was successfully launched on August 9th. It should be called Ceres-1B, because also the fairing was significantly changed for larger payloads.

2021:
The Ceres-1 is a four-stage rocket.
Three stages are operated with solid fuel, the fourth stage is a liquid maneuvering stage. The stage 1 and 2 are 1.4 m in diameter. The upper part of the rocket has a diameter of 1.2 m. The payload fairing has a diameter of 1.26 m and a length of 2.6 m. The maximum payload of the Ceres-1 is 350 kg.
The Ceres-1A (Y2) differs significantly from the Ceres-1 (Y1). The third stage now has the same diameter as the first two stages (1.4 m). The Y2 weighs 33 t (Y1: 31 t) and is ~1 m longer than the Y1. The maximum payload for a LEO increased from 350 kg to 400 kg.
