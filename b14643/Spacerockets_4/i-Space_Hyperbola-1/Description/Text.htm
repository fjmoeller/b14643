Hyperbola-1


The Hyperbola-1 launcher is a small solid fueled orbital rocket designed by the Chinese company Beijing Interstellar Glory Space Technology Ltd. under the brand name iSpace The first rocket has a maximum diameter of 1.4 m, a length of 20.8 m. Its liftoff mass is 31 t. Hyperbola-1 can carry a payload of 260 kg for a LEO (?).

For the second launch and so on, the diameter of the upper stages has been increased to 1.4 m. The length was increased to 24 m and the lift-off mass is now 42 t. The payload capability has been increased to 300 kg for a SSO.
                                                                                                         

2019, July 25 -- Successful maiden flight of Hyperbola-1 with three small satellites on board.The Hyperbola-1 consist of three solid-fuel stages and in addition a space tug with liquid-fuel. The rocket has a diameter of 1.4 m at base, a total length of 21 m, a takeoff mass of 31 t, a takeoff thrust of 555 kN and a capacity of 300 kg to low earth orbit.

2018, Sept. 05 -- The private Chinese company InterSpace (i-Space) sent a second suborbital rocket into space from the Jiuquan SLC.
The SQX-1Z rocket flew for more than 460 seconds and reached 108 kilometers above the ground.
The rocket carried three CubeSats for two Chinese commercial companies. After entering its preset altritude, the rocket will release two satellites for testing, and the third satellite will be parachuted to Earth.

2018, April 04 -- The 8.4 m long and 4.6 ton single stage test vehicle Shuang Quxian (SQX-1S) was launched from Hainan to an altitude of 108 km.
SQX-1S is a small solid fuel rocket, designed by the Chinese company InterSpace Technology Ltd., as a demonstrator for their planned Hyperbola-1 orbital rocket. InterSpace plans to perform the orbital launch by June 2019.
