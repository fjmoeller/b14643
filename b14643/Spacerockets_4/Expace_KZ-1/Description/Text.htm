Kuaizhou  KZ-1 & -1A
 
 

China Aerospace Science & Industry Corp.
 

Kuai Zhou (KZ) is a solid-fuelled launch vehicle family developed by the China Aerospace Science and Industry Corporation (CASIC). The basic variant Kuai Zhou launcher (KZ-1)  made a successful flight in 2013. CASIC is planning to introduce three Kuai Zhou designs for the commercial satellite launch market, including the 1.4 m-diameter KZ-1, the 2.2 m-diameter KZ-11, and the 3 m-diameter KZ-21.

 

2013, September 25 -- China launched a previously unknown quick response orbital launch vehicle on September 25, 2013 from Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center. A small satellite named "Kuaizhou-YZ" was inserted into a 300 km, 96.6 deg orbit.


The China Aerospace Science and Industry Corp. (CASIC) displayed a commercial model of its KZ-1 (FT-1) solid launch vehicle for emergency satellite launches at the 10th China International Aviation and Aerospace Exhibition (2014).

The FT-1 launcher has three solid-fuel stages and a restartable liquid-fuel upper stage which allows to launch several small satellites.It can lifting 430 kg into an orbit (incl. fourth stage), and then 200 kg into a sun-synchronous orbit. The complete vehicle is 19.4 m long and is carried on an extended-length semi-trailer with full-length upper doors.
CASIC does not acknowledge any military uses of the system or any connection with mobile intercontinental missiles. The company says that mobility allows the integration and launch sites to be separate. The system is still looking for an operational customer.
