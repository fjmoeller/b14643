NewLine-1
 
 


 

Link Space Aerospace Technology Group was established in January 2014. In January 2016, LinkSpace signed a strategic cooperation agreement on the commercial aerospace technology with Tenth Research Institute of China Aerospace Science & Industry Corporation (CASIC).
In mid-January 2016, LinkSpace and Fifth Research Institute 513 of China Aerospace Science and Technology Corporation (CASC) signed a strategic cooperation agreement on the development of commercial aerospace in Yantai, Shandong.
In future the LinkSpace NewLine-1 rocket will be a small Space Launch Vehicle (SLV) designed for microsatellite and nanosatellite launches. It will be capable of sending up to 200 kilograms into a Sun-synchronous orbit (SSO).
The launcher will have a takeoff thrust of about 500 kN. The first stage of the vehicle will consist of five methalox engines.
 

In July 2016, LinkSpace for the first time achieved the rocket hover technology through single engine vector control.
 

------------------------------------------------------------------

On March 27, 2019, First time LinkSpace successfully tested its SRV-T with five arranged small ethanolox engines in a free hopper launch.
The first launch of the SRV-1 with a new methalox engine LY-10 is planned for 2023.
The single-stage LN-1 test vehicle with five LY-10 engines is already in preparation. The LY-5A electric pump engine is under development for the second stage of the NL-1.
