Nebula-1
 
 
Beijing Deep Blue Aerospace Technology  Co., Ltd.
 

 Beijing Deep Blue Aerospace Technology Co., Ltd., was established in 2017 and is headquartered in Nantong City near the mouth of the Yangtze River.

The intention of this Chinese private company is to develop a reusable two-stage launch vehicle called Nebula-1. The first stage will have deployable landing legs and will use 9 Shenlan Leiting-20 engines.

With the 7.3 m high hopper "Nebula-M", some systems, e.g. landing legs and engine, were tested three times in flight.
Nebula-M1 used a small-thrust Leiting-5 engine with electric-pump-fed. The more powerful Leiting-20 engine, also with electric-pump- feed, was tested for the first time with the Nebula-M2.

The Nebul-M tests were part of the upcoming development of the 2.25-meter-diameter Nebula-1 orbital launcher, which is to be capable of lifting 500 kilograms to 500 km Sun-synchronous orbit.

In the future, two side boosters, identical to the first stage, will be added for a variant designated Nebula-1H.

 

Previous test flights (Nebula-M)
Nebula-M1: Launch on 30.07.2021. Altitude 10 m. Engine Leiting-5
Nebula-M2: Launch on 13.10.2021. Altitude 100 m. Engine Leiting-20
