IRI Qased
 
 

Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps  (IRGC)

 

2022, March 08 -- The second launch of a Qased with the Noor-2 satellite on board was successful. Orbit data: 495 - 513 km, inclination 58.3 °.

2020, April 22 -- An Iranian military satellite, mounted on a domestically-built rocket, was sent into space by the air arm of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC). Called Qased, the three-stage rocket blasted off from a TEL on the Shahrud launch site with the Noor-1 satellite on board. The launch has been completely successful and the satellite reached an orbit between 426 and 444 km altitude with 59.8° inclination.
 

Analysis (11.03.2022):
All previous analyzes have been wrong. The first stage of the Qased is exactly a Ghadr-H, here called "Sepehr". Similar to the Qiam-Scud, the oxidizer tank is probably divided in two separate tanks. Fairing and second stage have a diameter of 1.0 m. The second stage is a Salman TVC motor. The kick-stage has solid and liquid fuel and is integrated in the fairing. The Qased SLV is about 18.6 m long.
Part of the trajectory was decoded: The Ghadr-H stage had a reduced burning time of 20 seconds (2.34 t less fuel).The burning time of the Salman motor was about 70 seconds. The kick-stage is a solid-fuel motor "Arash-24" with a burn-time of 40 sec.
