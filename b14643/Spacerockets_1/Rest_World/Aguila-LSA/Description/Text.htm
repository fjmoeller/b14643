Aguila LSA
 
 

 


Argentina has two orbital LV projects. One is the hypergolic project form the civil space agency, CONAE, commonly known as the TRONADOR Project, or more correctly ISCUL (Inyector Satelital de Cargas Utiles y Livianas, or Satellite Injector of Useful and Light Payloads).

The second project is from the Ministry of Defense, it has a project to develop a "big" solid rocket ORBIT-II.
The first version will be called ORBIT-I, and will have active control. The rocket has like the Condor-I a diameter of 560 mm. The rocket will be the precursor model for the ORBIT-II LSA. According to the calculations, if they use four cores ORBIT-I as a first stage, the ORBIT-II will reach orbital heights (400km). The Military have the experience from the Condor-I and Condor-II missiles, and they want to use the single engine version for an interceptor up to 400 km, and with a multiple stage version that could put small satellites on LEO.
Many years after having de-activated the Condor missile program, now Argentina's military wants to recall the Condor ballistic system which was being developed by the Argentine Armed Forces. Argentina was forced to scrap the project in 1993.

But basically it were rockets with HTPB solid cores, with no thrust vectoring nor active surfaces, but it demonstrated successfully staging, and telemetry.
Another project is the sounding rocket FAS-1500 (Fenix) with four cores as first stage (thrust ~180 kN for 15 sec). All units are Canopus-II solid motors. The configuration is similar the Castor (AR) rocket in the late 60s.
