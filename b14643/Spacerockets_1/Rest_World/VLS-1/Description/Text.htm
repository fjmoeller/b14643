Veiculo Lancador de Satelites (VLS)
 
 

Instituto de Aeronautica e Espaco *  Centro Tecnico Aeroespatial *Agencia Espacial Brasileira

 

Brasilien startete seit 1965 fünf verschiedene Typen von Höhenraketen "Sonda". Die leistungsfähigste Variante war die zweistufige Sonda IV mit 7,3 t Startmasse. 1981 wurde beschlossen, aus der Sonda IV einen Satellitenträger zu entwickeln. Dazu wurde das Instituto de Aeronautica e Espaco (IAE) gegründet. Der  Laucher unter der Bezeichnung "Veiculo Lancador de Satelites" (VLS) wird seit 1978 im Centro Tecnico Aeroespacial (CTA) bei Alcantara gebaut. Bereits für das Jahr 1989 war der erste Start mit einem Satelliten geplant.
Die VLS-1 ist eine vierstufige Feststoffrakete mit eine Startmasse von 50 t. Sie soll Nutzlasten von 0,35 t in einen LEO bzw. 0,155 t in einen Orbit von 750 km transportieren können.
Als erste Stufe fungieren 4 Booster mit einem Schub von insgesamt 1.030 kN (s.l.). Die erste Stufe ist identisch mit einem Booster, aber mit einer größeren Ausströmdüse bestückt (ratio booster-nozzle 12.9:1 and  ratio core-nozzle  37.2:1). Die core-Stufe wird erst nach Abbrand der Booster gezündet.
Eine VLM (Veiculo Lancador de Microsatellites) wird für den Start von Satelliten <0,1 t vorgehalten. Bei dieser Variante sind die Booster entfernt, dafür wird eine kleine vierte Feststoff-Stufe hinzugefügt.
Der erste Start einer VLS-1erfolgte am 02.11.1997 vom Alcantara Launch Center, etwa 8 Jahre später als geplant, und wurde ein Fehlstart. Auch der zweite Start am 11.12.1999 schlug fehl. Nach Zerstörung der gesamten Infrastruktur bei der Explosion der dritten Rakete am 22.08.2003 wurde das Programm eingestellt.		   The VLS-1 is a four-stage, all-solid propellant launch vehicle with four S-43 motors as the first stage, one S-43 motor as the second stage, a S-40 motor as the third stage, and a S-44 motor as the fourth stage. the first three stages are controlled by nozzle gimbaling whereas the fourth stage is not guided.
Each  stage is powered by solid propellant motors of the same diameter and basic technology. Structure material of  S-43 and S-40 is 300M Steel, structure material of S-44 is Aramid/Epoxy.
During first-stage flight, the four gimbaled nozzles of the strap-on boosters provide three-axis attitude control. In the second and third stages, the nozzle performs pitch and yaw control, while a liquid propellant RCS provide roll control. The  RCS is contained in a bay on top of the third stage. The  fourth stage is spin stabilized.
The VLM is a another four-stage, all-solid propellant launch vehicle derived from the core stage of VLS-1. It is composed of a S-43 motor as the first stage, S-40 motor as the second stage, S-44 motor as the third stage, and new spin stabilized S-33 motor as the fourth (structure material Graphite/Epoxy).

 
VLS-1 is coming back ?


VLS-1
The Department of Aerospace Science and Technology held the first test of the new launch tower for the VLS-1 on the Alcantara Launch Center.
A life-size mockup of the VLS-1was attached to the tower, called TMI (Tower Mobile Integration).
The tower will undergo functional tests with the integration of mock-up of the VLS-1 inert (without fuel and satellite) during operation.
The integration step of the vehicle to the launch tower is part of Operation started in Salina Alcantara on June 20.
Tests are conducted by a team of 28 engineers and technicians from IAE (Institute of Aeronautics and Space), linked to the DCTA project and responsible for the VLS-1.
According to the IAE, the integration team performs the first functional tests of the vehicle in the new tower, from physical measurements and analysis, logic and mechanics.
In the new configuration of the VLS-1, the operation will enable the staff of IAE and position measuring all new vehicle connectors integrate them into the tower.
The mission being carried out is part of preparations for the first real test of the VLS-1 in flight, scheduled for 2013.
The flight of the rocket will be without payload (satellite) and serve as experts for the IAE verify the behavior of the thrusters (engines) of the first stage of the VLS-1.

XVT-01 will fly in 2013 and be renamed VSISNAV, since it will be used to test the indigenous INS (will use fiber optics gyroscopes). It will comprise first and second live stages only.
 Second flight on 2014 assuming there's budget for it.


VLM Nova
The VLM Nova (Veiculo Lancador de Microssatelites) based on a new S-50 motor is being studied, with the objective of orbiting satellites up to 150 kg in circular orbits ranging from 250 to 700 km.
VLM Nova is a three stage micro satellite solid LV developed together with DLR (Germany). First and second stage will be S-50, and third will be S-44 (later replaced with L-5 base stage). S-50 will be manufactured by CENIC (already does S-44) and will be composite. First flight is expected to be 2015 with SHEFEX-3 (suborbital).
Future looks to be VLS-Alpha and VLS-Beta, but going further makes no sense since it will overlap with Cyclone 4. They are re doing the long term plan.
