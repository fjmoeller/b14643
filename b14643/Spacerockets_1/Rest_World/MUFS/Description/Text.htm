Mikro Uydu Firlatma Sistemi MUFS
 
 

Roketsan A.S.

 

Roketsan has been continuing its efforts to develop a satellite launching system since 2007. Roketsan’s Department for the Design of Satellite Launching Systems set up in 2008 was the first department established in Turkiye within a defence company charged with undertaking projects concerning satellite launching systems. The Project (Period 1) that began in 2013 under an agreement concluded between the Presidency of Defence Industries (SSB) and Roketsan related to the development of a Satellite Launching System to meet a requirement of the Air Force Command was completed in 2014. Roketsan’s activities related to space are being carried out by the Space Systems Directorate established under the Department of Advanced Technologies and Systems Group founded in 2015.

Under a contract, signed between the SSB and Roketsan in 2018, the MUFS development project started, to put satellites with a maximum weight of 100 kilograms into a low earth orbit at a minimum altitude of 400 kilometers. In addition to the development of the Satellite Launching System, Roketsan will also build testing and production facilities as well as a launch base.
 

2021, Aug. -- A new version of the MUFS was presented at the IDF. The first stage was extended and likewise the new boosters with a diameter of ~ 0.90m.

 

2020, Aug. 30 --Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan announced in his speech during an inauguration ceremony of defense company Roketsan's production facility and research center in the Turkish capital Ankara

2019, April -- Roketsan has presented a model of its new MUFS (Mikro Uydu Firlatma Sistemi) at the IEDF'19 exhibition. The MUFS consists of solid-stages and with a liquid fuel upper stage, as it used for the earlier liquid fuel version.


2017, May -- Roketsan has presented his plan for the development of an orbital rocket. The UFA (Uydu Firlatma Araci) vehicle is two-stage and uses completely liquid fuel.


Note: So far, different versions of a UFA / MUFS are known. The model from 2020 seems to be a future hybrid version.
However, it is still a mystery what exact dimensions the presented versions of UFA or MUFS have. In addition, there is another future launch vehicle (UFS) of larger dimensions.
