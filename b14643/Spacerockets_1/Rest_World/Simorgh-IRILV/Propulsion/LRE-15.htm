How can be interpreted both pictures of an unknown Iranian rocket engine
(Simorgh first stage steering engine ?)

There are two published pictures of an unknown Iranian engine. An illustration is provided by Tal Inbar, Israel, and another was a long time ago (April 2012) on an Iranian forum presented (now untraceable). In the Iranian source of the image was reported that during the initial tests there were many malfunctions. Finally they could increase the burn time to 150, 320 and then 350 seconds. It was also reported that this engine is a newer second stage engine for the Simorgh !

Striking is a large combustion chamber and a typical old Isayev engines ripple structure on the nozzle. On the combustion chamber are devices for the pivoting of the engine mounted. The cooling system for the combustor is unique (2, 3). There are each a ring for the inflow and outflow of fuel mounted on the nozzle. It looks as if the combustion chamber is not regeneratively cooled because of outflow ring is located directly at the edge of the nozzle and connected is with the injector directly. However, it is likely that a part stream of the cooling flows upward through the wall of the combustion chamber.

        

Label on combustor: "Simorgh SLV steering engine combustor"


Are the presented steering engines
on the Simorgh the same design ?

I am not sure that this combustor is used as steering engine for the first stage of the Simorgh. A comparison with the images of the control engines at the Simorgh, which were presented in 2015, is not enlightening. But on the label is written "steering engine combustor". The only certainty is that the four steering engines of the Simorgh is connected with its own (fifth) turbopump (4, 5).

The performance of the engine is unclear. The sources mention permanently the term "attitude control engine" or "fifth engine" with a thrust of 15 or 13.6 tons for the Simorgh rocket. This meant of magnitude of about 147.0 or 133.4 kN thrust. So we assume that this engine a thrust generated of 4 x 33.34 kN (s.l.) and 4 x 36.77 kN (vac).

The dimensions of the combustor we can determine only in an indirect way, with the assumption that the label nearby the LRE-4 combustor is written on a paper in A6 format (1) and the label on the LRE-15 combustor also in A6 format (2).
