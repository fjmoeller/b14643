Zoljanah SLV
 
 

 Defense Ministry Space Group


2022, June 27 -- The launch of the "Zoljanah" was confirmed with a video. The exact date and the result are not known (failure?).
2022, June 14 -- The third launch of the Zoljanah is being prepared.(see "Launch History")


2021, February 01 -- An SLV, which is supposed to be "Zoljanah (WRS. Z0001)", was launched on the old Semnan launch site. The date of this likely unsuccessful launch is unknown.

2020 -- Gen. Hajizadeh, the Commander of Iran's Islamic Revolution Guards Corps Aerospace said that the Qased's liquid first stage was only used for testing efficiency and that future SLV launches will use a solid propellant first stage.

He added that the IRGC seeks to launch satellites in a higher orbit and with better performance in the near future.

2020, Aug. -- Defense Minister Hatami says *solid fuel* SLV Zoljanah is of the "same size and shape" as Simorgh SLV. Zoljanah can be launched from a mobile launch platform. Hatami mentioned that he thinks there will be a launch of the Zoljanah SLV in the current Iranian year (until March 2021).
Note: What mean "same size and shape" ?. It is puzzling. It cannot refer to the diameter.

Report, 2020, Feb. 09 -- Iran is planning to launch the Nahid-1 #2 satellite aboard a new, *solid fuel* SLV called Zoljanah. The launch will reportedly take place around summer 2020, and will send the satellite to ~250 km orbit.

TEHRAN, 2015, April 11 -- Deputy Head of the Iranian Science Ministry's Aerospace Research Center for Executive Affairs Mohammad Ali Farsi announced that the country has built two new space rockets named "Tir" and "Mehr".
The two rockets have been built at the space organization (of the Defense Ministry), Farsi told reporters in Tehran. Farsi said that the two rockets are now ready to launch into the space, adding that they could be sent into the space in two months at the demand of any research, academic or state center that intends to send satellites into orbit.

TEHRAN, 2015, Oct. 05 -- Head of Iran’s Aerospace Research Center Fathollah Ommi said two rockets are ready to be launched into space. Two rockets of "Tir" and "Mehr" are ready to be launched and can be sent into space anytime, he said.
