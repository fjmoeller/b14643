Iran's new liqid-propellant Kick-stage for its Zoljanah-2 SLV ?

 Completely out of focus so far was the fact that Iran has developed a liqid-propellant kick-stage, which was probably successfully tested for the first time at Qased in April 2020.

The first pictures of a model of this kick stage were presented in 2015 and later.

The stage is two-phase. The lower part consists (contrary to the previous assumption that it is a solid-fuel motor) of a ring-shaped tank (see detailed photo) with six spherical pressure gas bodies attached. The combustor is located in the hole of the tank ring, the long nozzle is outside at the lower end. The fuel combination is unknown (MMH / MON ?).

The second, detachable part of the kick stage is located above a grid adapter. It consists of four larger spherical tanks. The associated small engine is swiveling. Another six (?) mini-thrusters are distributed on the ring on which the spherical tanks are mounted. The type of propellant is unknown.
