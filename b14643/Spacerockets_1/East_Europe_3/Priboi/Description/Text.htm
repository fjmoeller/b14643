Priboi (R-39/R-29)
 
 

Makeev State Rocket Center
 

The Priboi launch vehicle is being created on the basis of technologies and components of the existing submarine-launched ballistic missiles (SLBM). For the first stage the components of the R-39 solid-propellant missile are used, for the second and third stages - components of the R-29SM (Shtil) liquid-propellant missile. The fourth and fifth stages are intentionally being developed with the use of technologies of the same missiles.
The launch vehicle is delivered to the ship divided on three units: a solid-propellant stage with launch tray, a cluster of liquid-propellant stages and a payload section. The surface vessel has a docking chamber which is used to assemble the launch vehicle and to evacuate it on the water surface by means of a floating anchor. Hermetically sealed areas under fairing and in launch tray provide positive floatage of the launch vehicle in the horizontal position. After the transfer of the launch vehicle from the horizontal position into free-sailing state in the vertical position the launch tray is separated and the first stage engine is initiated. The launch command is initiated from the vessel.

The Priboi-M launch vehicle is being created on the basis of R-39 and R-29RM solid- and liquid-propellant engines which have been developed and have passed a series of tests.
The launch vehicle consists of three-stage solid-propellant carrier and liquid-propellant booster.
The tail section of the first stage is modified to enable its joining with the launch system elements.
The third stage of the launch vehicle is joined with the booster through a newly developed interstage section.
For operation of the booster in cruise mode (post-injection maneuver) the third stage engine of the R-29RM (Shtil) is used. Trajectory correction and positioning on the orbit are performed by the dispensing engine of the R-39. After being fueled at the factory the engines are encapsulated and after that do not require any servicing. Engine unsealing and tank pressurization are performed during flight by means a self-contained pressurization system located in the interstage section.
The upper stage includes a nose fairing and adapter for a spacecraft installation. The nose fairing comes in three versions. To conduct a launch from a water surface a hermetically sealed nose fairing is being developed; for any other launch conditions the dust- and moisture-proof fairing is developing. The nose fairing is jettisoned upon the separation of the second stage.

The Priboi-U launch vehicle is being created on the basis of engines of the serial production R-29RM. The rocket has four main stages and a booster and has a tandem/strap-on configuration (a core unit and two strap-on boosters).
The core unit is a modified Shtil-3 missile (it has fastening elements for joining of strap-on boosters).
Strap-on boosters are the first stage of the Priboi-U and are being developed on the basis of the first stage engine of the R-29RM (Shtil). The core unit differs from the R-29RM missile by the shape of oxidizer tank upper bottom, which has spherical shape (the base version has a sphero-conical bottom).
The strap-on boosters are mounted in parallel to the core unit in stabilization planes II-IV. The upper part of each booster is covered by a taper fairing.
The Priboi-U launch vehicle can be launched from the ground-based launch pad using infrastructure of the existing spaceports.
