Air Launch "Polyot"
 
 

Air Launch Aerospace Corporation (ALAC)

The Russian Air Launch Aerospace Corporation (ALAC) was established as a successor to a 1997 joint task group (Polyot Aviation Company and KB Khimavtomatiki) to explore the possibility of creating an air-launched system capable of placing satellites into LEO using the Antonov An-124 "Ruslan" as a flying launch pad.
The Russian Air Launch Aerospace Corp. has given a commercial presentation of its commercial launch system, called Polyot, which will deliver 3.2–3.7 tonne payloads into Low Earth Orbit after being dropped from an Antonov An-124 transport plane. The rocket will be powered by an NK-43M (high-altitude version) first stage engine.
The Air Launch System incorporates:
Antonov An-124-100AL carrier aircraft (CA) as a flying launch pad;
Polyot two-stage Launch Vehicle (LV) with modified LOX/Kerosene NK-43M first stage engine and RD-0124 second stage engine; modified RD-0158 LOX/Kerosene upper stage.
The Air Launch project development is carried out by top-level Russian aeronautical and rocket-and-space companies with the participation of the Ukrainian Antonov Aeronautical Scientific/Technical Complex (ASTC).
The Air Launch System's major designers are:
ASTC - development of aviation systems, including the carrier aircraft (CA) and CA ground maintenance and preparation facilities;
State Rocket Center Academician V.P. Makeev Design Bureau - development of LV systems, space systems and mission control center, including LV, USB and ground mission preparation and operation facilities;
State Research & Production Space Rocket Center TsSKB-Progress - development of the second stage (modified Block I stage of the Soyuz-2 launch vehicle) and launch vehicle manufacturing;
Kuznetsov Scientific & Engineering Center - NK-43 engine modification into NK-43M first stage sustainer;
KB Khimavtomatiki - RD-0124 engine adaptation to the Polyot second stage operating conditions and development of the RD-0158 upper stage engine;
NPO Avtomatiki - development of the Polyot control system (based on the Soyuz- 2 control system);
KBTM - development of ground and airborne mission preparation and operation facilities. NPO Lavochkin, JSC Motorostroitel, UAPK Aviastar, NTP Novag and other Russian aerospace companies will also take part in project development.

 

Another project to launch satellites into low orbit from the Vozdushny Start Corporation is planned. The project involves the launch of two-stage rockets with payloads of up to 3.5 tonnes from An-124-100 Ruslan heavy transport planes.
The first stage of the rocket would use a NK-33MN engine. The second state will use a modified version of the engine, which has for a long time been used in the Block-DM unit produced by RKK Energia. Both engines use kerosene and liquid oxygen.
Vozdushny Start was founded by the Production Association Polyot (Voronezh), RKK Energia (Korolyov, Moscow region), Antonov Aviation Complex (Kyiv), the Pilyugin Institute (Moscow) and Progress Rocket-Space Center (Samara).
Air Launch Corporation had formalized agreements between its major participants in the period from August 1999 to March 2000. The corporation declared itself "ready for the job" in July of that year. Among its major members are Polet Airlines, Korolev's Rocket and Space Corporation “Energia” (RKK Energia), ANTK Antonov, TsSKB-Progress (Samara) and Pilugin's scientific-production center, KB Khimavtomatiki, Moscow Energy Institute, Aviastar factory, Nikolai Kuznetsov Design Bureau, KBTM (TransMash Design Bureau) and JSC Motorostroitel. 
The Air Launch two-stage rocket booster should be capable of placing a 3300 kg payload into low orbit with an inclination of 63 degrees. The rocket will use well-tried components, such as the NK-33MN and 11D58M first and second stage engines respectively.
 

The August 4, 1999, decision of TsSKB Progress to develop an Air Launch aircraft-rocket system was a turning point in the life of the Yamal family. The corporation got an about 40% stake in Air Launch. In the framework of the project, a two-stage Polyot should be developed to be launched from the An-124-100 Ruslan aircraft. The first stage (Block 1) will have one NK-33MN engine. Compared to NK-33M, its thrust should rise from 188 to 196 tonnes-force. The Yamal stage or Block 2 (modified Proton upper stage with RD-58MFD engine) should serve as its second stage.
