Chang Zheng      CZ-FH (CZ-5DY)
 
 

China Academy of Launch Vehicle Technology (CALT) / Shanghai Academy of Science & Technology

 

 

 CZ-FH (CALT) -----> CZ-5DY -----> CZ-5G
Core and boosters are each equipped with engines YF-100K It is planned to add 2 boosters. Two upper stages follow. The top stage, like the CZ-5, is equipped with three YF-75D engines.
The new Crew Transport System for long missions weighs 14 tons and, together with the service module, is 7.23 m long. For the lunar mission, the service module is modified for more power.

The research, started in 2017, has produced an initial blueprint for the rocket, which will be about 87 meters long and have a takeoff weight of 2,211 tonnes.A dual launch could do a Lunar mission, sending the LM first to LLO or NRO, followed by the next generation crewed vehicle.
The current plan seems to be aiming at around 70 tonnes LEO for the 2 stages version and 27 tonnes to LTO for the 3 stages version.
1st stage: Core + 2 booster of 5.0 meters diameter, each with 7 x YF-100K engines with 1,230 kN of thrust (total 21).
2nd stage: 2 x YF-100M (vacuum version of YF-100K).
3rd stage:  3 x YF-75D (LH2 engine currently used for CZ-5 2nd stage)

 

CZ-FH (SAST)

Alternatively, there is a proposal for a CZ-FH from SAST, which apparently was not accepted. The first stage consists of a bundling of 4 CBS with a diameter of 3.8 m with 5 YF-100K engines each (20 in total). Two more stages follow with YF-100M and YF-75D engines.


"In October 2018, the China Academy of Launch Vehicle Technology unveiled a model of a conceptual design for a human-rated launch vehicle at the 12th China International Aviation and Aerospace Exhibition in Zhuhai, southern China.The design uses clusters of already-developed YF-100K kerosene- and liquid-oxygen engines and 5-meter-diameter cores like those of the Long March 5, making its development, if approved, much faster, potentially providing China with the launch capabilities to carry out human lunar exploration of the moon much sooner than previously expected.
A previous mission concept for putting Chinese astronauts on the lunar surface required the Long March 9, a super-heavylift launcher with a 10-meter diameter that China aims to fly in 2028. This profile also includes the lofting of the crew via a CZ-5 and an Earth-orbit rendezvous."
