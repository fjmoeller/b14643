Sodruzhestvo SHLV
 
 

 

Moscow, 2012, August 20
Russia's Rocket and Space Corporation “Energia” will take part in a tender for development of a new heavy-class carrier rocket.
Energia build the new superheavy carrier rocket in cooperation with Ukraine and Kazakhstan. The new Sodruzhestvo rocket will have two-stage and three-stage variants. The Sodruzhestvo rocket has to have the capacity of 60-70 tons to lift a 20-ton space vehicle to the orbit at the height of 200km.

The tender calls for the draft of the rocket’s design to be ready by May 31, 2013.

Russia’s space agency Roscosmos recently announced a tender for design of a carrier rocket capable of delivering a large variety of spacecraft to low- and high-orbits, including sending manned spacecraft to a Lunar orbit.

Launches of the new rocket will be carried out from the Vostochny space center currently under construction in the country's Far East.

“RSC Energia has the most technical and design solutions ready to carry out this project,” Energia's president and general constructor Vitaly Lopota told reporters at the International Aerospace Congress in Moscow.
“It is possible to create the new rocket at such a rapid pace only in one case: if we using the already existing flight components”, he explained. In particular, Lopota mentioned the rocket engine RD-171 used in Zenit rockets”.

"The Energia project includes the launch system, which is still viable. At least it can be brought back to life. It also includes the "A" rocket units, used in the Zenit carrier rocket, and also engines made today. We proposed using oxygen-kerosene engines instead of the hydrogen central part (of the Yenisei HLV project), and make the first and second stages of five units," he said.
