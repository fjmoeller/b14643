"On April 22, 2019, Head of Roskosmos Dmitry Rogozin met with foreign diplomats at the Cosmonautics and Aviation center of Moscow's exhibition grounds, VDNKh, where he made a presentation on the status of the Russian space program, including a vision for the prospective launch vehicle fleet.
The rocket fleet presented by Rogozin included three members of the Angara family, including Angara-1.2, Angara-5/5M and Angara-5V, along with the existing Briz-M and Block DM-03 space tugs, as well as the yet-to-be-built KVTK upper stage. The brand-new Soyuz-5 (Irtysh) launcher would come next with its capability to carry a new-generation crew vehicle.
The two variants of the proposed super-heavy rocket included the Yenisei and Don architectures. The newly released data mostly confirmed the previously known specifications of the planned super rockets but also added several additional data points.
However, still unclear, was the actual funding available for this ambitious program. At the time, only Angara-5 and Angara-1.2 were approaching production after nearly three decades of planning and 15 years of active development." (Anatoly Zak)

 

Note: This includes also the Volga (ex Zenit-3F)
