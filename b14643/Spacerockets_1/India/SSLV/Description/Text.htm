Indian Space Research Organisation * Hindustan Aeronautics Ltd.

 

 

2022, July -- The SSLV's maiden flight took place on August 07 as planned. The two satellites did not achieve a stable orbit because the VTM was not working. There may also have been trouble with separation from the third stage.

 

2018, January -- The first of Indian Space Research Organisation’s (ISRO) "baby rocket" in the making is likely to fly in the sky in the first half of 2019, top officials of ISRO’s Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC) said.
The SSLV consists of three solid fuel stages and a bi-propellant RCS/Velocity trimming module. It has a principal diameter of 2 m and a length of 34 m with a lift-off mass of ~120 t.
 The SSLV will place small satellites in the low LEO.

 

Note: The launcher is based on the Agni-V missile
