H-IIB
 
 

JAXA * Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Ltd.
 

 

Japan is planning to develop a 5-m-diameter twin engine first stage with 4 SRB-A's which is designated H-IIA 3040 (or H-IIB).
The H-IIB launch vehicle is a two-stage rocket using liquid oxygen and liquid hydrogen as propellant and has four strap-on solid rocket boosters (SRB-A) powered by polibutadiene.
The H-IIB is currently under joint development by JAXA and MHI as an enhanced version of the H-IIA, the present flagship rocket. The H-IIB, featuring two LE-7A rocket engines for the first stage firing (single LE-7A engine for the first stage firing of the H-IIA), larger fuel tanks for the first stage engines (tank diameter expanded from 4 to 5.2 meters). The total length of the first stage is extended also by 1m from that of H-IIA. At the result of such enhancement, the H-IIB needs propellant 1.7 times more than the former. The launcher will have a maximum launching capacity of 8-ton payloads, twice that of the H-IIA.
The H-IIB is expected to be deployed for launching the H-II Transfer Vehicle (HTV) that will transport materials to the international space station ISS. It will also enable launching of multiple satellites with a single rocket, and is envisaged to enhance the H-IIB's competitiveness in the satellite launch market by largely reducing launching costs.

Note: The H-II Transfer Vehicle (HTV) is designated to transport up to 6 tons of supplies to astronauts in the space station, including food, clothing and experimental equipment.
