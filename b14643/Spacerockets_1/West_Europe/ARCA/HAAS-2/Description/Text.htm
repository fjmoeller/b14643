ARCA HAAS-2
 
 

Asociatia Romana pentru Cosmonautica si Aeronautica (ARCA)

 

ARCA presents  in Bucharest, its new HAAS-2 orbital rocket.
HAAS-2 will be launched from the ground and will have as objective the flight testing of the Executor engine and to reach orbit. The mission will also be a final test for the Moon launch of the European Lunar Explorer space probe, in the Google Lunar X Prize american competition.
The HAAS-2 rocket will be one stage launched, being for the first time when is attempted to launch a satellite by a one stage rocket. This is possible because of the rocket tanks' construction technology of composite materials that are very light.

ARCA and SIAD Romania became partners in the Romanian Space Program.
SIAD Romania will provide over the coming years liquid oxygen for the Executor rocket engine, the "heart" of the entire ARCA Space Program. SIAD Romania will also provide ARCA with liquid oxygen systems and with the expertise required to deploy and operate these facilities.
Liquid oxygen, a cryogenic gas, with a working temerature on board the tanks made by ARCA of - 180° C, is used with kerosene to fuel the rocket engine. Kerosene burns inside the engine, in an oxygen environment, with the temperature reaching values above 3000° C, the resulting gases allowing the engine to provide a traction of 24 tons.
SIAD Romania is not only a supplier of industrial gases and technologies, but also an active and consistent participant with its partners in various projects and new applications. Therefore, we are very glad that ARCA has given us the opportunity to participate in the Romanian Space Program.

Now ARCA is named as ARCA Space Corporation with its base in Las Cruces, New Mexico.

The HAAS-2C is an orbital rocket intended for commercial payload launches. There are two variants of the rocket, a single stage to orbit variant capable of placing a 50 kg payload into orbit and a two stage variant capable of lifting a 400 kg payload into orbit. After testing the extremely lightwight composite tank, ARCA designed a single stage 18m long rocket with a total weight of 510 kg, having a thrust to weight ratio of 26:1 and a 50 kg payload. They presented the rocket in Victoria Square in Bucharest, in front of the Romanian Government building. The second stage version will be powered by the Executor engine for the lower stage and the upper stage will use a smaller engine adapted for vacuum, named Venator.

The new HAAS-2B is a changed single stage suborbital rocket. It can transport a crew capsule and service module into suborbital flight. The crew capsule and service module are the same used for the larger multi-stage Super HAAS orbital rocket. At NASA DC-X conference Alamogordo, New Mexico in August 2013 ARCA presented an updated version of HAAS-2B rocket with a capsule capable of carrying a crew of five into space. There were discussions with Spaceport America representatives to operate HAAS-2B rocket from New Mexico.

The Executor Rocket Engine (ERE) is an open cycle gas generator rocket engine for the first stage of the HAAS rocket.

In order to reach the objectives, the team decided to use composite materials and aluminum alloys on a large scale. The composite materials offer low construction costs and a reduced weight of the components.
Composite materials were used in the construction of the combustion chamber and the nozzle, and also the gas generator and some elements in the turbopumps.
The combustion chamber and the nozzle are built from two layers. The internal layer is made of silica fiber and phenolic resin, and the external one is made of cabon fiber and epoxy resin.The phenolic resin reinforced with silica fiber pirolizes endothermally in the combustion chamber walls, releasing gases like oxygen and hydrogen, leaving a local carbon matrix. The gases spread through the carbon matrix and reach the internal surface of the wall where they meet the hot combustion gases and act as a cooling agent. Furthermore, the engine is equipped with a cooling system that injects on the internal walls 10 percent of the total kerosene mass.
The gas generator has the same cooling principle and also the intake and exhaust turbine volutes, that are constructed from the same composite materials mentioned above.
Using these light materials makes the Executor engine to have a thrust/mass ratio of 110. The most performant European engines used for Ariane and Vega launchers do not reach a ratio of 100.
The pump volutes are made of 6062 type aluminum alloy. The pump rotors are made through lathing and milling using 304 type steel. The supersonic turbine is made of refractory steel, both the core and the blades. The turbine rotation speed is 20.000 rpm and has a 1,5 MW power. The intake gas temperature is 620 degrees Celsius.
The main engine valves are made of 6060 type aluminum and are pneumatically powered, without adjustement. The gas generator valves are ball typed, pneumatically powered and can be adjusted, being one of the few elements not entirely made at ARCA.
The engine injector and the liquid oxygen intake pipes are made of 304 L type steel and the kerosene intake pipe is made of composite materials.
The engine has the posibility to direct the thrust by 5 degrees on two axis. The articulated system is made of composite materials and high grade steel alloy. The engine is rotated using two hydraulic pistons that use kerosene from the pump exhaust system. The hydraulic pistons are made of steel and are made entirely at ARCA.

The Venator Rocket Engine (VRE) will be used to power the second stage of the HAAS-2C rocket.
Venator is pressure feed rocket engine, that uses liquid oxygen and kerosene and has a maximum thrust of 2.5 tons of thrust.
The main objectives for this engine were low development and construction costs, without sacrificing the high performance.
In order to reach the objectives, the team decided to use composite materials and aluminum alloys. Excepting the injection head and liquid oxygen pipe, almost all engine components are made of composites. The composite materials offer low construction costs and a reduced weight of the components.
The combustion chamber and the nozzle are built from two layers. The internal layer is made of silica fiber and phenolic resin, and the external one is made of carbon fiber and epoxy resin.The phenolic resin reinforced with silica fiber pirolizes endothermally in the combustion chamber walls, releasing gases like oxygen and hydrogen, leaving a local carbon matrix. The gases spread through the carbon matrix and reach the internal surface of the wall where they meet the hot combustion gases and act as a cooling agent. Furthermore, the engine is equipped with a cooling system that injects on the internal walls 10 percent of the total kerosene mass.
The Venator rocket engine has no vanes on the main pipes. It uses instead burst disks on the main pipes, between the tanks and the engine. The second stage is pressurized at 2 atm at lift-off and after the first stage burn-out, the second stage will be pressurized at 16 atm. At that pressure the disks will burst and the fuel will flow through the engine. Using burst disks, the mass of the stage will decrease while the reliability will increase.
The pump volutes are made of 6062 type aluminum alloy. The pump rotors are made through lathing and milling using 304 type steel. The supersonic turbine is made of refractory steel, both the core and the blades. The turbine rotation speed is 20.000 rpm and has a 1,5 MW power. The intake gas temperature is 620 degrees Celsius.
The engine injector and the liquid oxygen intake pipes are made of 304 L type steel and the kerosene intake pipe is made of composite materials.
The second stage is spin stabilized at 60 rpm, immediately after staging. The spin will be created using four helium RCS.
