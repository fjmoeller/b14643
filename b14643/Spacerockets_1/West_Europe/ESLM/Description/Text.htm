European Small Launcher Medium (ESLM) --------> BB-PPH

 The Future Launchers Preparatory Program (FLPP) of the European Space Agency (ESA) is preparing the next generation of launchers to be developed in Europe. The FLPP is a system driven programme that targets the long term applications, with as well possible short-mid term spin-offs. Its scope is threefold: launch vehicles system studies, in-flight experimentation and on-ground maturation of technologies.
Three expendable launcher options are identified by ESA as potential solutions to ensure an independent access to space for Europe in the decades to come:
  • Evolution of the existing European launchers. The Ariane 5 major evolution, that may be operational in 2016, would consist in an advanced cryogenic upper stage utilizing an engine building on the FLPP expander cycle demonstrator program
  • A new expendable mid-term launch system relying on elements or “Building Blocks” from Ariane and Vega to be operational by 2015
  • A new long-term launch system called the Next Generation Launcher (NGL) to be operational by 2020-25.

The BB-PPH launcher is composed of:
A first stage derived from the large solid propellant booster of Ariane 5, i.e. the MPS. The casing using the Filament Wound (FW) technology while the motor itself is overloaded with respect to MPS up to 250 tons of propellant.

A second stage derived from the first stage of VEGA launcher: P80. The motor has a FW casing and is slightly overloaded w.r.t. P80 to 110 tons.

The Cryogenic Upper Stage the architecture is derived from present Upper Stage of A5ECA, with a higher loading: 28 tons. The engine using expander cycle is able to deliver 180 kN in vacuum conditions.

With a height of 62 m and a GLOW of 430 tons,this launcher will be able to deliver 3 tons in GTO orbit in its linear configuration. The implementation of two (respectively six) strap-on boosters of 35 tons of propellant each would allow increasing the GTO performance up to 5 tons (resp. 8 tons).
To serve the institutional need (e.g. LEO, MEO, SSO missions) which will request a multi-boost scenario for the Upper composite, two options were investigated:
Cryogenic Upper Stage would be designed in order to be re-ignitable.
Terminal boost will be carried out by a Kick-stage employing MMH/NTO propulsion technology.
