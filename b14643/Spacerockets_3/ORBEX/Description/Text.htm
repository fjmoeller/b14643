ORBEX Space Ltd.

 

The British company ORBEX Space has released new details about a small satellite launch vehicle it expects to have ready in the next few years, part of a growing number of ventures seeking to build and launch rockets from the country.
In a presentation at a Royal Aeronautical Society commercial space conference on Nov. 22 (2017). OREX said the company was making good progress in the development of a launch vehicle designed for launching small satellites of 165 kilograms into polar orbits from a launch site on the north coast of Scotland.

Orbex has been focusing on the development of a 6,750-pounds-force engine, uses propane (biopropane) as the core fuel.
The specific impulse of propane is also slightly higher than RP-1. One key aspect of propane is that it remains liquid at cryogenic temperatures. That enabled a “coaxial tank” design for Prime launcher where a central tube of propane is surrounded by an outer tank of liquid oxygen, creating structural mass savings in the rocket.

The "Prime" rocket is 17 metres tall and about 1.3 metres in diameter. The first launch is planning for the second half of 2021.
