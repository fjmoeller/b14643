Taimyr NLV
 
 

LIN Industrial & LAROS

LIN Industrial wants to create a series of small carrier rockets "Taimyr" with capacities from 10 to 180 kg for an earth orbit. The various rockets are assembled from standard components URB-1, DRM-1, URB-2 and URB-3.

The universal missile block URB-1 of first/second stages use an engine of ~ 4000 kg thrust (initial version with 9 engines and thrust of ~ 400 kg). An URB consists of the transfer compartment, the instrument compartment, the tank of compressed helium intertank compartment unit control engines on a cold gas, an oxidizer tank intertank compartment, fuel tank and tail section, which houses propulsion .
The related DRM-1 (sustainer) uses the same engine but with larger nozzle.

URB-2 and URB-3 consists of the instrument compartment, fuel tank, intertank compartment in which are installed two tanks of compressed helium, oxidizer tank and tail section with the main engines and engines control unit on the cold gas.

Taimyr-1A - a three-stage rocket. Mass - 2.6 tons, length - 16 m, weight of the payload to low Earth orbit - 12 kg.
The first stage - URB-1 with nine engines of ~ 400 kg thrust, the second stage - URB-2 engine thrust ~ 400 kg, the third stage - URB-3.

Taimyr-1 - a three-stage rocket. Mass - 2.6 tons, length - 16 m, weight of the payload to low Earth orbit - 14 kg.
The first stage - URB-1 with one engine of ~ 4000 kg thrust, second stage - URB-2 engine thrust ~ 400 kg, the third stage - URB-3.

Taimyr-5 - a three-stage rocket. Mass - 11.2 tons, length - 16 m, weight of the payload to low Earth orbit - 108 kg.
The first stage - 4 URB-1 with one engine of  ~ 4000 kg thrust, second stage (sustainer) - DRM-1 with  engine of ~ 4000 kg thrust, the third stage - URB-2 engine with a thrust of ~ 100 kg.
 
Taimyr-7 - a three-stage rocket. Mass - 15.6 tons, length - 16 m, weight of the payload to low Earth orbit - up to 180 kg, into sun-synchronous orbit - 85 kg.
The first stage - 6 URB-1 with one rengine of thrust ~ 4000 kg thrust, second stage (sustainer) - DRM-1 with engine of ~ 4000 kg thrust, the third stage - URB-2 engine with a thrust of ~ 100 kg.

                                                                                                                                     

2016, April 12 -- LIN rocket engine with a thrust of 100 kg is assembled and ready for testing.
The first instance of our liquid-propellant rocket engine, powered by kerosene and high-test peroxide, is assembled and ready for firing on a test stand at MAI.
The engine is designed for proving the basic design and technology solutions on a test stand. The test firings are expected in 2016.
The engine burns stable high-boiling propellants.

Primary specifications:
Propellant: Oxidizer — PV-85 (stabilized high-test peroxide, 85%); Fuel — TS-1 (jet fuel grade)
Thrust: Sea level — 100.0 kg;  Vacuum — 120.0 kg
Specific impulse: Sea level — 1840 m/s; Vacuum — 2200 m/s
Flow rate: Oxidizer — 0.476 kg/s; Fuel — 0.057 kg/s
Propellant mixture ratio (O:F) — 8.43:1
Gas pressure: Combustion chamber — 16; Nozzle exit — 0.7
Inner diameter of the engine: Cylindrical section — 80.0 mm; Nozzle exit — 44.3 mm

2016, Dec.11 --  LIN Industrial has got an explosion during their peroxide engine test a couple of days ago. This was the very first test firing by Lin Industrial. It looks like they have had two successful ignitions of their HTP/Kero engine RLD-100S with some burn before the accident.
