Black Arrow-2
 
 

Black Arrow Space Technologies * Horizon Sea launch
 

 

The Black Arrow 2 will be a two-stage initially all expendable rocket using Liquid Oxygen (LOX) and Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG) which is mainly methane as propellants. Standing nearly 25m tall, it is a two stage orbital space launch vehicle designed to reliably deploy small satellites in support of missions for the UK Space Agency (UKSA), European Space Agency (ESA) and commercial satellite companies from all around the world.
The 1.8 m diameter first stage will use a single 500 kN thrust gas-generator cycle pump fed engine while the upper stage will be powered by a 45 kN pressure fed upper-stage engine.

The initial version of the Horizon Space Techologies’ Black Arrow 2 is being designed to lift at least 500 kg to 200×200 km equatorial orbit, or 350 kg to 600×600 km sun-synchronous orbit.

New:

Black Arrow 2
The Black Arrow 2 two-stage launch vehicle will be designed and manufactured in Oxfordshire.
The vehicle will be constructed with leading edge composites, using patented techniques, developed and refined with expertise from successful Formula 1 teams.
Propellant and other pressure tanks within the vehicle will be designed and constructed by Astrotanks Company using advanced manufacturing techniques.. Fully composite replacements will appear a short time.
The rocket will be powered by proven engine and propulsion technologies, adapted for use in the Black Arrow 2 launch vehicle, comprising innovative 3D printed parts, and using cryogenic Liquified Natural Gas (LNG) and Liquid Oxygen propellants.. Five engines will provide ~450 kN thrust to the first stage, with added reliability and recovery function, with a single-engine propelling the upper stage.

Black Arrow Space Technologies will launch payloads of up to 500 kg into Polar Low Earth Orbit, or up to 300 kg into Sun Synchronous Orbit, from a unique seaborne launch vessel.
The service will be marketed under "Horizon Sea Launch".
