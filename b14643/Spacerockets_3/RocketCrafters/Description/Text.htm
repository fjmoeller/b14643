Intrepid-1
 
 

Rocket Crafters Inc. (RCI)
 

 

Rocket Crafters, based in Titusville, is developing Intrepid-1, a revolutionary two-stage to orbit space launch system. Intrepid-1 will be the world’s first launch vehicle that is constructed from 3D printed carbon fiber composites and powered by D-DART, a breakthrough in advanced hybrid rocket engine technology.

Intrepid-1 will be the first in a family of this revolutionary new hybrid rocket powered expendable orbital launch vehicles designed specifically to affordably and responsively deliver small spacecraft to customer defined orbits.

Beginning with Intrepid-1, with first launch planned for Q1 2019, Rocket Crafters plans to develop XL and XL+ variants.

RCI is developing high-performance Hybrid Rocket Engines for integration with Intrepid-1’s 1st and 2nd stages.
RCI owns the exclusive rights to the patented Hybrid Rocket invention created by the Company. This rocket technology represents a true paradigm shift in the Rocketry field. D-DART, the tradename for RCI’s proprietary Hybrid rocket technology, is the first Hybrid rocket system consistent and reliable enough to be clustered. D-DART’s throttle-able and restart capable rocket engines can be scaled and tailored quickly and affordably to meet the most stringent thrust profile and mission requirements.

Intrepid-1 specification

16.2m Stack height; 1.7m diameter; 24,200kg GTOW (Gross Take-Off Weight)

Stages:
1: 4 x 82 kN Sparta-82B hybrid engines
2: 4 x 3 kN Sparta-3V hybrid engines

Capability:
376kg to 500km Sun Synchronous Orbit
250kg to 750km Polar Orbit
220kg to 750km Sun Synchronous Orbit
