Spectrum
 
 

Isar Aerospace Technolgies GmbH
 

Spectrum  is a orbital launcher of the German  space company Isar Aerospace Technologies GmbH. It is intended for the launch of small satellites. A first flight is announced for 2021. The launch site is not known yet. It could be a platform in the North Sea.
Isar Aerospace was founded in March 2018 by space engineers Daniel Metzler, Markus Brandl and Josef Fleischmann. The company is based in Gilching near Munich. The founding members were previously active in the Scientific Association for Rocket Technology and Space (WARR), a student group at the Technical University of Munich, which had already developed their own engines and a suborbital missile. Isar Aerospace is supported by the start-up funding program of ESA.
In a first round of financing in the summer of 2018, the heating manufacturer Viessmann and the venture capitalist UVC Partners, a company of two former employees of the space company SpaceX participated.

Spectrum specifications:
Length: 27 m; diameter 2 m
Propellant: Liquid propane and liquid oxygen
Fairing:: 2.0 x 6.0 m
Payload: 1000 kg to LEO or 700 kg to SSO


First stage
The first stage features nine Aquila SL engines. Clustering the same engine enables extensive flight heritage thus allowing for continuous upgrades  and making the propulsion system more reliable.
Thrust (s.l.) 675 kN; Burn time 140 s

Second stage
Spectrum's second stage is able to inject payloads directly into orbit. Having multi-ignition capability, satellite constellations can be deployed without the need for an  additional kick stage. The stage features a single Aquila VAC engine.
Thrust (vac) 94 kN; Burn time 230 s
