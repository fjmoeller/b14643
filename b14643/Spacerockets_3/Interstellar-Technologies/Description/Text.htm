Momo
 
 

Interstellar Technologies Inc. ( I ST )

 

 Momo is a Japanese sounding rocket capable of delivering 20 kg to a height of 100 km. It is privately developed by Interstellar Technologies Inc. (IST).
The single stage Momo is powered by a pressure-fed engine of 12 kN and fueled with ethanol and liquid oxygen. The engine is gimbaled for steering, with cold gas thrusters providing roll control. The rocket has a diameter of 0.50 m and a length of 9.9 m. The lift-off weight is 1.15 tons (about 0.80 t propellant).
The rocket is manufactured and launched at Taiki-cho, Hiroo-gun, Hokkaido.

The first rocket (Momo-1) was launched on 30 July 2017, but the flight was terminated 70 seconds after engine ignition.

The second flight (Momo-2) was launched on 29 June 2018. This time the engine cut off after four seconds and the vehicle fell back to the pad and exploded.

The third launcher (Momo-3) launched successful on 04 May 2019 and reached an altitude of 113.4 km.

The fourth launch (Momo-4) is failed on 27 July 13, 2019. The problem was the onboard computer.

The Momo-5 launch on 13 June 2020 ended in a failure by anomaly of the propulsion.

The Momo-7 launch on 03 July 2021 with the new motor (v1) was successful. The rocket reached an altitude of 99 km.

The Momo-6 launch on 31 July 2021 with the new motor (v1) was successful. The rocket reached an altitude of ~90 km.

 

Zero is the future launch vehicle for transporting satellites into orbit. The first stage is equipped with new LNG / LOX engines of 60 kN thrust each.
