Skyrora Ltd.
 

2020, May 20 -- The company has successfully static tested a fully integrated, sub-orbital Skylark L launch vehicle in flight configuration. This means all actions of a launch have been performed, but did not release the vehicle. The rocket engine successfully burned, with all vehicle systems showing nominal operation
Skylark-L is a bi-liquid propellant launch vehicle and is Skyrora’s first sub-orbital flight vehicle, ready to reach a height of approximately 100km and carry a payload of up to 60kg. Skylark-L uses a propellant combination of Hydrogen Peroxide and Kerosene which are pressure fed into a Skyrora 30kN engine.
Building up to the static fire test, the rocket engine itself has gone through three hot fire tests before integration into the vehicle. When commercial, the company plans to use their own Ecosene, an equivalent Kerosene fuel made from un-recyclable plastic waste.
 


Skyrora-XL

 Skyrora Limited is a UK company headquartered in Edinburgh, producing launch vehicle technology inspired by Black Arrow.

"We aim to reduce the cost of space launches through the combination of proven technology and advanced engineering methods. We have already started developing a sounding rocket to test our technology. As part of that process, we are building a robust supply chain focused on UK companies, creating new employment opportunities throughout our industrial sector.
We aree planning to test our engines in Westcott, which played an important part in the Black Arrow project. Like us, Black Arrow used hydrogen peroxide as its oxidiser and we're proud to be following in its footsteps 50 years on.
When it comes to selecting a launch site, we believe there is nowhere better than Scotland.
In the meantime, we will keep building and testing our rockets, helped along by Skyrora's Ukrainian R&D team located in Dnipro boasting decades of experience and detailed knowledge. With their support, we will achieve our aims to produce a cost effective launch vehicle that combines the best of British industry with practical experience. Our ability to leverage talent and experience from our Ukrainian specialists is the key to our continued growth and success."

Specifications of Skyrora XL

FIRST STAGE
Propellant components: Hydrogen Peroxide and Kerosene
Thrust per engine: 70 kN (at sea level), 79.7 kN (in vacuum)
Exhaust Velocity: 2,454 m/s (at sea level), 2,794 m/s (in vacuum)
Specific impulse: 250.24 s (at sea level), 284.91 s (in vacuum)
Gross mass at launch: 44,611 kg
Dry mass of the stage: 2,600 kg
Total propellant mass: 32,065 kg
Engine type: 9 x liquid propellant rocket engines

SECOND STAGE
Propellant components: Hydrogen Peroxide and Kerosene
Thrust (in vacuum): 85.5 kN
Exhaust velocity (in vacuum): 3,001 m/s
Specific impulse (in vacuum): 306.02 s
Gross mass: 9,921 kg
Dry mass of the stage: 1,070 kg
Total propellant mass: 7,664 kg
Engine type: 1 х liquid propellant rocket engine with uncooled nozzle
