Atlas-Vega
 
 

 

 American orbital launch vehicle. Atlas-Vega consisted of an Atlas LV-3C booster with a storable propellant upper stage. It was planned by NASA at its inception for deep space and planetary missions before the Atlas Centaur was available. Work had already begun when NASA discovered that the US Air Force had an essentially identical launch vehicle (Atlas-Agena) in development for the highly classified Corona reconnaisance satellite program. Atlas-Vega was accordingly cancelled. The development ended 1959.

 

Grand Central GE-405H Vega Upper Stage Engine
Propellants: LOX/RP-1
Thrust (vac): 33,840 lbf (150.5 kN)
ISP 300 sec
Expansion Area Ratio (Ae/At): 25
Notes: Developed from the earlier 405 engine and intended for the Atlas/Vega interim upper stage which was to be used before Atlas/Centaur became operational. First flight was scheduled for 1960, but the Atlas/Vega SLV was cancelled in favor of Atlas/Agena SLV. NASA decided to continue the 405H’s ground testing after Vega’s cancellation, and the engine was operated for over 2,000 seconds in 12 consecutive static firings without component change or repair.
