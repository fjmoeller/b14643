Space Launch System (SLS)


       

  Dynetics proposal for a liquid-fuel booster application  for Block-2

Dynetics, Inc. and Pratt & Whitney Rocketdyne (PWR) formed a team “to offer an affordable booster approach that meets the evolved capabilities of the SLS. The proposed booster features a robust structural design based around an 18 foot diameter core, paired with two F-1B engines, evolved from the most powerful RP/LOX engines ever flown.
While the SLS Block 2 – with advanced Solid Rocket Boosters – is estimated to provide a capability of 130mt to orbit, Dynetics claim that by using the vehicle assumptions for the fully evolved SLS, their proposed booster delivers 150mt.
