Exos SARGE
 
 

Exos Aerospace Systems and Technologies
 

Two years after Armadillo Aerospace suspended operations, the new Exos Aerospace company featuring many of the same people is planning to resume development of its reusable suborbital launch vehicles.

In August 2013, Armadillo founder John Carmack announced that the company had gone into “hibernation” after the last STIG-B launch because of a lack of funding.
Exos Aerospace has evidently purchased some or all of Armadillo Aerospace's equipment and facility and have an ambitious plan to build 4 suborbital rockets within the year, and begin development of a manned rocket during that time frame. Development of an orbital rocket is also planned.

Exos Aerospace Systems and Technologies of Caddo Mills, Texas, is planning to begin launches of a reusable sounding rocket called the Suborbital Active Rocket with Guidance (SARGE) from Spaceport America in New Mexico.

SARGE is an upgraded version of Armadillo’s last vehicle, called STIG-B. Exos Aerospace expects to have SARGE ready for its first test flight as April/May 2017 from Spaceport America.
