History of Chinas's YF-21/24 rocket engines

 

The YF-20 is a Chinese liquid rocket engine burning N2O4 and UDMH in an gas generator cycle. It is a basic engine which when mounted in a four engine cluster forms the YF-21. The high altitude variant is known as the YF-22 and is  paired with YF-23 verniers to form the YF-24 propulsion module for second stages. Over time several improvements to the engine performance has been achieved. Finally, the engine YF-24 of the second stage was in 1991 for the first time replaced by a significantly upgraded engine version with an extended nozzle on the main engine (CZ-2E), and later (1999) were also added new Verniers. Obviously, the engine YF-24 replaced by the engine YF-24E for all versions since the year 2002.
